## Research
### Judgment, Evaluative Schemas, and Entrepreneurial Idea Generation [[here]](https://www.startiq.org)
with Aaron Chatterji, Sharique Hasan, and Rick Larrick, Working Paper (June 2022)


**Abstract**

Most entrepreneurial ideas are of uncertain quality. As a result, many entrepreneurs pursue ideas bound to fail. Recent work in strategy argues that this uncertainty can be mitigated through the ex-post testing of ideas using mechanisms such as the scientific method, A/B testing, or feedback from other ecosystem members such as incubators, venture capitalists, or peer entrepreneurs— often referred as “online testing”. How- ever, these mechanisms are costly or inaccessible to most entrepreneurs, thus limiting their broader impact on entrepreneurial ecosystems. In this article, we propose and empirically evaluate the efficacy of a less costly ex-ante mechanism for evaluating en- trepreneurial idea quality—improving the evaluative schemas used by entrepreneurs to structure the judgment of idea quality and test more ideas “offline”. Specifically, we test whether training entrepreneurs to use formal schemas to judge new business ideas leads to (a) improved judgment of new venture idea quality, (b) the ability to generate higher-quality business ideas, and (c) better calibration in predicting which of one’s ideas are higher quality. We conducted a pre-registered experiment with students at an elite MBA program to test our theory. Our results show that participants in our treatment group have (1) a 17.6% improvement in their ability to predict new venture ideas; (2) generate ideas that are about 15% higher in quality; and (3) better anticipate which of their ideas are high- or low- quality. We validate and extend our results by externally rating the quality of the ideas generated with a panel of evaluators. The results imply that many—if not most—entrepreneurs lack rigorous evaluative schemas that help them judge the quality of their ideas. Simple training can improve idea quality and may complement existing ex-post methods by reducing the costs of testing ideas.
